About Boobak Village

History • Culture • Education • Community

Introduction

Boobak is a historic and culturally rich village located in District Charsadda, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan. The village is known for its educational institutions, religious services, hospitality, and strong community values. Over the years, Boobak has produced respected scholars, teachers, public servants, and community leaders.

Origin of the Name "Boobak"

According to local traditions, the name "Boobak" originated from a large pond that once existed in the area. Shepherds used to bring buffaloes for bathing. When the buffaloes moved in the water, waves would hit the edges of the pond creating "Buk Buk" sounds. This is how the name Boobak was formed.

Geographical Location

Boobak extends from Motorway Bridge to the border of Hindukalay. The area includes Dera Qai, Government High School regions, graveyards, and surrounding settlements.

Conclusion

Boobak represents a unique blend of history, education, religious learning, and cultural heritage. Boobak is a small rural village located in Charsadda District of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan. It lies near the town of Nisatta, which is only a few kilometers away. The village is part of the fertile Peshawar Valley region, known for its rich agricultural land. According to the 2017 census data, Boobak has an estimated population of around 6,086 people, making it a relatively small settlement rather than a town or city. The majority of the residents are engaged in farming and agriculture, which forms the backbone of the local economy. Life in Boobak is strongly community-based, with deep-rooted traditions, cultural values, and close family structures that continue to shape daily living. At the same time, the village continues to preserve its traditions while contributing to the development of future generations through education and gradual modernization.
